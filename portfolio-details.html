OdiArt Museum — Digital Catalog & Exhibit

November 01, 2025 Photography • Curation • Web Purvasha Folk & Tribal Odi Art Museum

For the OdiArt Museum I documented exhibits, prepared a digital catalog and set up on-site display systems. The project combines cinematic photography and practical tech solutions so visitors can engage with tribal art both in-person and online.

Project Overview

The goal was to preserve and present local folk and tribal art through high-quality images, easy-to-navigate web presentation and kiosk displays inside the museum. Work included photography, image optimization, light retouching, responsive web gallery integration, and simple on-site digital signage setup.

Key Highlights

  • Cinematic photography & image optimization for web
  • Responsive portfolio viewer with gapless full-screen pages
  • On-site display setup and content management for the museum
  • Lightweight viewer allowing easy PDF/PNG updates via GitHub
  • Short training for museum staff to update content

Technical Implementation

Images were optimized (WebP fallback), lazy-loaded for fast browsing, and served from the repository using a simple viewer that reads sequentially named pages (page-1.webp, page-2.webp...). On-site displays used local playback and a mirrored web-view for remote updates.

"Saurav’s photography and setup brought our collection to life online — visitors now interact with our exhibits in ways we never imagined."

Curator, Purvasha OdiArt Museum
Museum Management

The result is a visually consistent, easy-to-maintain digital catalog that complements the physical exhibition and provides a fallback archive for the museum’s collections.
