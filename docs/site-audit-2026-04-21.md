# Website Audit — dearlipu.github.io
Date: 2026-04-21

## 1) Positioning audit (what to improve first)

### What is already strong
- Clear value proposition in hero: strategy + execution for design/video/motion.
- Good breadth of service coverage.
- Contact flow is practical (WhatsApp + form fields + direct email).

### Biggest positioning gaps
1. **Primary CTA is weak in the hero**
   - You show social icons, but there is no dominant action like:
     - “Book a discovery call”
     - “Get a quote in 24 hours”
     - “View 3 case studies”

2. **Proof is mostly generic, not verifiable**
   - Stats and testimonials are present, but they read as template-like and don't tie to named projects.
   - Your strongest conversion asset should be before/after and measurable outcomes.

3. **Too many sections for a first scroll-through**
   - Current order includes: Hero, About, Services, Projects, Pricing, Testimonials, Contact, Brands.
   - This is comprehensive, but heavy for decision-making users.

4. **Navigation labels are internally focused, not buyer-focused**
   - “About / Services / Projects / Pricing / Reviews / Contact” is standard, but can be clearer as:
     - Work
     - Services
     - Process
     - Pricing
     - Start a Project

## 2) What to remove (or move lower)

## 3) Section-by-section guidance (your “What I Do” and “Selected Work” confusion)

## A) “What I Do” (Services section) — how to use it
Use this section to help visitors self-qualify quickly.

**Format that converts better:**
- Service name (1 line)
- Best for (1 line)
- Typical deliverables (3 bullets max)
- Starting timeline (e.g., 3–7 days)
- Micro CTA (“See related work”)

**Example card format:**
- **Social Content Design**
  - Best for: creators and brands needing weekly content consistency
  - Deliverables: post designs, carousels, thumbnails
  - Timeline: 3–5 days for first batch
  - CTA: “View social content case study”

> Goal: services should reduce confusion, not show everything you can possibly do.

## B) “Selected Work” — how to use it
This is your most important section. It should prove outcomes.

**Replace generic project entries with mini case studies:**
Each card should include:
1. Client type/industry
2. Problem
3. What you delivered
4. Result metric (watch time, CTR, saves, leads, sales, etc.)
5. “See full breakdown” CTA

**Good structure (3 featured works only):**
- Project 1: Social growth system
- Project 2: Video retention improvement
- Project 3: Brand refresh + conversion assets

Then add “View all projects” link to separate page.

## 6) Research notes from similar sites/services

### Observed from the provided reference (portavia.framer.website)
- Uses a familiar one-page flow: hero → what I do → about/stats → featured projects → testimonials → FAQ.
- Heavy use of sections and repeated content blocks can feel template-like if not deeply personalized.
- Strength worth copying: clear segmentation of services and visible featured projects.
- Weakness to avoid: duplicate/generic content and placeholder-style data.

### Observed trend in portfolio templates/services
- Most high-conversion freelancer templates emphasize:
  - case-study-first storytelling
  - concise service packaging
  - visible CTA near top
  - social proof near relevant work items (not isolated only in a carousel)
